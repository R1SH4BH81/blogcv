What are Neural Networks?

At its core, a neural network consists of interconnected nodes or neurons. These neurons process and learn from data, extracting identifying features without pre-programmed understanding. Unlike traditional algorithms, neural networks adapt and improve their performance over time.

How do Neural Networks Work?

1. Neurons and Connections : Neurons receive inputs, which are governed by thresholds and activation functions.Connections involve weights and biases that regulate information transfer between neurons.

2. Learning Process : Neural networks learn through iterative refinement.The process involves input computation, output generation, and adjusting weights and biases.

3. Three Stages of Learning
Input Computation: The network processes input data.
Output Generation: The network produces an output based on the computed data.
Iterative Refinement: The network adjusts weights and biases to enhance its proficiency.

How do Neural Networks Work?

3.Recommendation Systems: Neural networks learn user preferences and recommend personalized content.Used by platforms like Netflix and Amazon.
